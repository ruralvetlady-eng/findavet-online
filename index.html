When to go to the ER

  • Trouble breathing, collapse, or unconsciousness
  • Severe bleeding or suspected broken bones
  • Poison/toxin ingestion (chocolate, lilies, xylitol, meds, rodenticides)
  • Persistent vomiting/diarrhea or bloated, painful abdomen
  • Seizures, heatstroke, or extreme lethargy
  • Urinary blockage signs (straining/no urine), especially male cats
  • Serious trauma, bites, or eye injuries

If you’re unsure, seek care now or call a clinic.
